About Us

A social enterprise for honest honey

a·pi·ar·y — noun. A place where bees are kept; a collection of beehives.

We are a social enterprise aiming to address the proliferation of adulterated honey in the Philippines. We help honey lovers — especially diabetic patients and nutritionists who care about sugar levels — source pure, unadulterated honey from credible sources.

🍯

The Problem

Naturally farmed honey takes a long time to harvest, so some farmers unethically feed bees sugar syrup instead of natural nectar. Bees don't convert syrup into another product — what they are fed is what they produce.

❤️

Effect on Health

Over 3.7 million Filipinos are diagnosed with diabetes. Many turn to honey as a healthier sweetener — not knowing that honey from syrup-fed bees carries high sugar levels unsafe for their consumption.

🐝

Effect on Bees

Bees' health suffers when they're fed sugar syrup — threatening the world's best pollinators with decline and extinction. See why bees matter

Best Seller

Honey

A sweet, sticky material made by bees from flower nectar. High-quality honey is rich in antioxidants — swapping refined sugar for pure raw honey can bring enormous health benefits, boosting the brain and lifting fatigue.

Why Bees Matter

The importance of bees in our ecosystem

Pollination is one of 17 recognized ecosystem services. Over 75% of the world's most important crops — and 35% of food produced — depend on animal pollination.

🍎

Our food depends on them

Apples, pears, cucumbers, cherries, melons, cranberries, asparagus and broccoli all rely on bees to fertilize their flowers. Blueberries and cherries are 90% dependent on honey bee pollination — almonds depend on it entirely.

🌳

Ecosystems are built on them

As pollinators, bees support the growth of trees, flowers, and plants that feed and shelter creatures large and small — interconnected ecosystems that let diverse species co-exist.

☕

Coffee & biofuel too

Coffee is pollinated by bees. So is canola — a key ingredient in next-generation biofuels. The decline of bees would ripple through industries far beyond the dinner table.

🐄

Even livestock feels it

Oilseeds — grown only from bee-pollinated plants — are cattle's main protein source. Without bees, meat production goes downhill along with fruits and vegetables.
