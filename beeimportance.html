Importance of Bees in Ecosystem

Crops consumed by humans are also dependent on bees. For example, apples, pears, cucumbers, cherries, melons, cranberries, asparagus, broccoli and other flowering/fruiting crops depend on bees to fertilize the flowers so that fruit will develop. When a bee gets nectar from a flower, it gets coated in pollen. The bee then carries this pollen to another flower, fertilizing it and causing the flower to develop into a fruit. Without pollination, many flowering crops would not yield fruit, or would yield very little.

As pollinators, bees play a part in every aspect of the ecosystem. They support the growth of trees, flowers, and other plants, which serve as food and shelter for creatures large and small. Bees contribute to complex, interconnected ecosystems that allow a diverse number of different species to co-exist.

Pollination is one of 17 recognized ecosystem services. The production of over 75% of the world's most important crops and 35% of the food produced is dependent upon animal pollination. The services provided by pollination have significant economic value as well. In the EU, for example, insect pollination has an estimated value of 15 billion euros per year.

As honey bees gather pollen and nectar for their survival, they pollinate crops such as apples, cranberries, melons and broccoli. Some crops, including blueberriesand cherries, are 90-percent dependent on honey bee pollination. One crop, almonds, depends entirely on the honey bee for pollination at bloom time.

Like crops, coffee is also pollinated by bees.
